Economics
meets computation.

I am a PhD student in Economics and M.Sc. student in Computer Science at Louisiana State University. My research sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence and economics — studying how data, algorithms, and intelligent systems reshape markets, organisations, and decision-making.

My background spans mechanical engineering, business administration, and now economics and computer science — an unusual path that shapes how I think about problems.

Research
2021
Published
Resource Planning System for Organisations: A Soft Computing Approach
Erfan Zarafshan, Sina Gholami, Reza Sheikh, Shib Sankar Sana
International Journal of Services, Economics and Management, Vol. 12(3), pp. 272–293
Develops a soft computing framework for organisational resource planning, combining fuzzy logic and heuristic methods to model uncertainty in service systems — offering a tractable alternative to rigid optimisation in incomplete-information environments.
2021
Published
The Analysis and Interpretation of Quality Cost Using Rough Set Theory: A Case Study from the Ceramic Industry
Erfan Zarafshan, Samira Rezaei Toroghi, Reza Sheikh, Shib Sankar Sana
International Journal of Service and Computing Oriented Manufacturing, Vol. 4(1), pp. 11–31
Applies rough set theory to analyse quality cost structures in manufacturing. Using ceramic industry data, the paper extracts decision rules supporting quality management under real-world data imprecision.

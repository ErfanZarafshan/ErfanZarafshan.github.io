02 · Background & Academic Journey

About Me

I am a PhD student in Economics and M.Sc. student in Computer Science at Louisiana State University's E. J. Ourso College of Business. My research sits at the frontier of economics and computation — using methods from machine learning, rough set theory, and data science to address questions in business economics, decision science, and AI markets.

My academic trajectory is deliberately interdisciplinary. I began with a B.Sc. in Mechanical Engineering at Babol Noshirvani University of Technology, Iran, which gave me rigorous quantitative and systems-thinking foundations. I then pivoted to management and social science, earning an M.Sc. in Business Administration from Shahrood University of Technology, where I began applying soft computing to economic and operational problems — producing peer-reviewed publications cited over 30 times.

At LSU, I am simultaneously pursuing a PhD in Economics and an M.Sc. in Computer Science — building the dual expertise needed to work rigorously on the economics of artificial intelligence, algorithmic decision-making, and data-driven policy analysis.

My published work has accumulated 32+ citations and 1,400+ reads on ResearchGate, and I am actively developing my doctoral research agenda on AI economics and its implications for markets, organisations, and public policy.
